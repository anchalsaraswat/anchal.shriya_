Introduction

Los Angeles is the second most populous city in the United States, and is known as the commerical center of Southern California. It's known for its mediterranean climate and it's entertainment industry and is a must-visit destination mainly because of it's diverse selection of food and culture.Los Angeles has countless activities for everyone to choose from. With 5 major theme parks including Disneyland and Universal Studios, as well as nearly a dozen smaller ones. There are over one hundred museums, dozens of zoos and aquariums, and 10 professional sports teams which call the area home. If that’s not enough for you, there are also over 8300 restaurants, nearly 400 bars and night clubs, and countless concert venues which always have something fun and exciting going on.It is a city that is about opportunity, creativity, culture and experience.

Beaches

Los Angeles County has over 70 miles of coastline, and it’s all beautiful. Some tourists flock to the populated shores of Santa Monica or Venice Beach for people-watching and sight-seeing, while others prefer the more demure and secluded beaches of nearby Malibu. Orange County has gorgeous beaches too, and it’s just a short car or train ride away.A very popular beach for tourists is Sunset Point Beach which is known as a great spot for novice surfers.The rest of the state beach offers volleyball and a more typical sandy Socal sunbathing beach.

Food

Los Angeles is home to so much good food that even year-round residents can’t consume it all. Those looking for fine dining may try Providence, helmed by Chef Michael Cimarusti, Beverly Hills’ Spago or Santa Monica’s Mélisse. For more modern spots, there’s Hot Hot Food, which specializes in numerous types of fried rice, Silver Lake Thai favorite Same Same, or Echo Park’s elegant Winsome. There’s also all of Thai Town, where people hail Jitlada as the champion of spice. Trendy outposts such as Salazar in Silver Lake (the shrimp quesadilla is amazing) or roadside carts or hidden backyard gems such as Dona Mary in Watts serve up excellent Mexican fare.These are just some examples of the food you get to eat. New restaurants are opening up every week, meaning the scene is constantly evolving—just like your palate.
